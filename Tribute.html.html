About Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

  • Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, India, was a prominent social reformer, jurist, economist, and politician. He is best known as the principal architect of the Indian Constitution and a lifelong advocate for the rights of the marginalized and oppressed, particularly the Dalits (formerly known as "untouchables").

  • As the Chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Indian Constitution, he played a crucial role in framing the document, ensuring it provided comprehensive rights and protections for all citizens, especially the socially disadvantaged. Dr. Ambedkar's vision for an inclusive India laid the foundation for a nation committed to social justice and equality.

  • Throughout his life, Dr. Ambedkar fought tirelessly against social discrimination and worked to uplift the oppressed. His contributions extend beyond the Constitution, including efforts to promote education, economic reform, and the eradication of caste-based injustices.
